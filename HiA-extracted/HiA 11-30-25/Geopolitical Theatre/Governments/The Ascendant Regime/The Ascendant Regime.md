# The Ascendant Regime

## 1. Overview
The Ascendant Regime is a centralized political authority based in Scale Port, seeking to unify all settlements of Kael under a single ideological and administrative system. It positions itself as the steward of Kael’s stability and claims legitimacy through mastery of infrastructure, skyflow routes, and civic order.

## 2. Government Structure
- City-state authoritarian hierarchy  
- Central Council of Elevation presiding over high-level decisions  
- Enforcement branches responsible for internal cohesion  
- Administrative governors assigned to aligned or conquered settlements  

## 3. Ideology & Goals
- Promote unification of Kael’s settlements under standardized governance  
- Expand the influence of Scale Port across regional boundaries  
- Maintain stability through controlled growth and regulated skyflow travel  
- Assimilate divergent local customs into a unified civic identity  

## 4. Territorial Influence
- Full control of Scale Port and its immediate surroundings  
- Expanding influence into major trade-linked settlements  
- Strategic outposts positioned along key skyflow corridors  
- Resistant regions include isolated or culturally strong settlements  

## 5. Population & Demographics
- High concentration of Kaelborn citizens  
- Presence of mixed Elementari, Monstrakin, and other species  
- Diversity centered around trade and transport hubs  

## 6. Economy & Resources
- Controls major skyflow routes and port infrastructure  
- Access to maritime-equivalent trade via airborne channels  
- Economic leverage through taxation, tariffs, and resource coordination  

## 7. Military or Defense Structure
- Structured defense network around Scale Port  
- Skywatch divisions monitoring atmospheric activity  
- Ground detachments stationed in aligned settlements  

## 8. Relations with Other Powers
- Rivalries with autonomous regions valuing independence  
- Fragile truces with Enclaves and Stoneclans  
- Strategic pressure on resource-rich or skyflow-critical zones  

## 9. Pressure Points & Weaknesses
- Overextended supply lines into distant or isolated settlements  
- Resistance movements rooted in cultural or environmental identity  
- Limited ability to project power into extreme biomes  

## 10. Current Tensions
- Growing unrest in frontier settlements  
- Cultural pushback from non-centralized communities  
- Strain between expansion agenda and geographic limitations
